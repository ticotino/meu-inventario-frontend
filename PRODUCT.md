# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary user is a solo workshop owner (or a very small production team) running their own manufacturing business. They move between raw-material stock, production runs, customer orders, and shipping manifests throughout the day, often in short check-in sessions rather than long sessions at a desk.

## Product Purpose

Meu Inventário connects the full production chain — matérias-primas (raw materials), produção (production), pedidos (orders), and romaneios (shipping manifests) — into one continuous, trustworthy record, with fabricantes (manufacturers/suppliers) tracked alongside. Success looks like never being surprised by a stock-out, never losing track of an order, and trusting that what the app shows matches physical reality.

## Positioning

It's built for this exact workflow — matérias-primas → produção → pedidos → romaneios — rather than a generic inventory tool the owner has to bend to fit their process.

## Brand Personality

Fast, clear, no-nonsense. The owner checks or updates stock and gets back to work — the interface should get out of the way rather than perform. No strong reference points to imitate or avoid; the bar is a clean, uncluttered, purpose-built tool.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS inventory-dashboard clichés that don't reflect this specific workflow: decorative hero-metric cards with no real data behind them, dense enterprise data-grids built for teams of analysts, and anything that adds ceremony (extra clicks, confirmation modals, decorative motion) to a five-second stock check.

## Design Principles

One continuous record: every screen should reinforce that raw materials, production, orders, and shipments are one connected chain, not siloed lists that happen to share a nav bar.

Fast in, fast out: day-to-day usage is short and frequent — minimize clicks and friction for the common actions (checking stock, logging a movement, confirming an order).

Built for this workflow, not generic: resist pulling in generic inventory-SaaS patterns that don't match the matérias-primas → produção → pedidos → romaneios shape of this specific business.

Trustworthy at a glance: numbers and statuses are the owner's source of truth — legibility and accuracy come before visual flourish.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard practice: solid color contrast, fully keyboard-usable forms, readable text sizes. No specific WCAG target or known accessibility need beyond that.
